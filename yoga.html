Origin

How It Began

In 2021, after years of probing the nature of intelligence through transistors and training algorithms, a different kind of question arose: What is the experiencer of all this computation? What is the perceiving subject that my research was so carefully circumnavigating?

That question led me to Yoga — not as fitness, but as a rigorous system of inner science. In 2021, I took my first steps into Hatha Yoga and meditation, and something shifted irreversibly.

What I found was not mysticism, but methodology. Yoga offered a first-person laboratory for the very questions neuroscience and AI approach from the outside in.

Philosophy

Yoga as Experiential Science

For me, Yoga is not separate from research — it is research, conducted from the inside. Here is how they inform each other:

  • The observer problem in AI. Every neural network requires a loss signal — a reference point. Yoga asks: what is the reference point in human experience? Who or what is the "loss function" of consciousness?
  • Stillness as ground state. Deep meditative states reveal a baseline of pure awareness — analogous to the quantum vacuum, a state of zero excitation from which all experience arises.
  • Precision in the body. Classical Hatha Yoga cultivates extraordinary sensitivity to subtle internal states — a precision instrument of self-study unavailable to third-person science.
  • Perception before cognition. Yogic inquiry distinguishes raw perception from conceptual overlay — mirroring the neuroscientific distinction between primary sensory cortex and higher association areas.
  • Non-dual awareness. Advanced contemplative states challenge the subject/object duality fundamental to classical physics — and invite new models of information and consciousness.
  • Integration, not conflict. The rigour of science and the depth of inner inquiry are not in tension. They are complementary epistemologies — two lenses on the same reality.
